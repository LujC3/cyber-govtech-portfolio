======================================================================
  EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
  Cybersecurity Risk Assessment
  Philippine Component City — Local Government Unit
======================================================================

Date Prepared   : May 2026
Prepared For    : Office of the City Mayor / City Administrator
Classification  : CONFIDENTIAL

SCOPE
  This assessment covers 39 identified cybersecurity risks
  across 15 information assets spanning 11 city departments.

HEADLINE FINDINGS
  • 6 risks rated CRITICAL requiring immediate action
  • 22 risks rated HIGH requiring short-term remediation
  • 6 risks directly tied to Data Privacy Act compliance
  • Average risk score: 13.8 out of 25

MOST EXPOSED DEPARTMENT
  MIS Division — cumulative score 137

PRIMARY RISK DRIVERS
  1. No Data Protection Officer appointed (DPA violation)
  2. No security awareness training for employees
  3. Outdated/unpatched systems across all departments
  4. Single point of failure in backup infrastructure
  5. Default credentials on network and IoT devices

RECOMMENDED BUDGET PRIORITY
  Allocate cybersecurity line item in next Annual Investment
  Programme (AIP). Estimated minimum: PHP 2-5M for immediate
  controls (email security, endpoint protection, backup).
